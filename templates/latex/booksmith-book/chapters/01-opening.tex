\chapter{The Seed Vault}

Mira held the lantern low enough for the glass trees to catch fire in its
reflection. Every branch answered with a small, impossible sunrise.

The map in her palm did not name the orchard. It named the people who had been
buried beneath it, one root at a time.

Eli stopped at the last stair and pressed two fingers to the wall. The stone was
warm. That was wrong. The greenhouse above them had frozen every window white,
and the orchard was supposed to keep its secrets below forty-one degrees.

``You knew this was here,'' Mira said.

``I knew there was a lower room.'' His voice stayed soft, which meant he was
afraid. ``I did not know it had a door.''

The door waited at the end of the passage, black iron veined with silver. A
single word had been carved into the lintel in pre-collapse script. Mira could
not read it, but the letters made her teeth ache.

Behind the door, something tapped once against glass.
