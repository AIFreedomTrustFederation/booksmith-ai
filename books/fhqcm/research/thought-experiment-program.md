# FHQCM Thought Experiment Program

## Purpose

A rigorous theory needs thought experiments that clarify assumptions, expose contradictions, and reveal testable implications.

FHQCM should use thought experiments in the spirit of disciplined physics and philosophy: not as proof, but as tools for conceptual precision.

## Thought Experiment Standard

Each thought experiment must include:

1. Setup
2. Assumptions
3. Variables involved
4. Expected result under ordinary interpretation
5. Expected result under FHQCM interpretation
6. What would count against FHQCM
7. What remains philosophical or symbolic
8. Related figure ID
9. Required citations

## Research Rule

No thought experiment should be used to overstate evidence. Its job is to make a question sharper, not to force a conclusion.
