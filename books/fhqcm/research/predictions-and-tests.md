# FHQCM Predictions and Tests Registry

## Purpose

This file records candidate predictions that could eventually make parts of FHQCM empirically testable.

These are not finished scientific claims. They are research leads that require operational definitions, controls, measurement protocols, and statistical review.

## Candidate Test Areas

### P001 — Cross-Scale Coherence

Hypothesis: conscious regulation practices produce measurable coherence across breath, heart rhythm, neural activity, and behavior.

Possible measures:

- heart-rate variability
- respiration rhythm
- EEG coherence
- behavioral timing
- self-report integration scales

Possible falsifier: no difference from baseline or known mechanisms fully explain the results.

### P002 — Recursive Narrative Patterns

Hypothesis: unresolved trauma, recovery, and transformation show measurable recursive narrative patterns over time.

Possible measures:

- linguistic recurrence analysis
- theme repetition
- sentiment change
- moral inventory structure

Possible falsifier: no stable recurrence structure appears beyond ordinary language patterns.

### P003 — Relational Coherence

Hypothesis: emotionally attuned groups show increased physiological or behavioral synchrony compared with non-attuned groups.

Possible measures:

- HRV synchrony
- speech timing
- gesture timing
- cooperative task performance

Possible falsifier: synchrony is not greater than control conditions.

### P004 — Attention and Perception Integration

Hypothesis: disciplined attention practices change how participants integrate memory, present perception, and future anticipation.

Possible measures:

- attention tasks
- reaction time
- memory recall
- future planning tasks
- phenomenological reports

Possible falsifier: no measurable difference after intervention.

## Warning

A measurable effect does not automatically prove FHQCM. It may support a limited sub-claim while alternative explanations remain possible.
