# 008 — Christic Equivalency

Figure ID: F038

## Purpose

Help readers understand the model's use of the equal sign as a symbol of reconciliation, not as a literal collapse of all distinctions.

## Plain-Language Setup

Imagine two realities that seem opposed: wave and particle, spirit and matter, past and future, self and other. FHQCM asks whether these pairs can be understood as complementary expressions held together by a deeper coherence.

## Reader Should Notice

- Equivalency does not mean sameness.
- Reconciliation preserves distinction while healing division.
- The equal sign is symbolic, theological, and philosophical in this context.

## Relation to FHQCM

This is the heart of Christic harmonic equivalency: the divine principle that restores divided realities into communion.

## Limits

This is not a physics equation. It is symbolic language that must be framed as theological and philosophical interpretation.
