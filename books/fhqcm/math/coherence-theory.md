# Coherence Theory

## Definition

Coherence is perceived relational integration among events, meanings, observers, and systems.

Decoherence is perceived fragmentation or separation relative to the observer's scale and frame.

## Symbolic Model

```text
K = sum R(e_i,e_j)
```

where K is coherence and R is perceived relational participation.

A simple complement model may be written as:

```text
D = 1 - K
```

where D is decoherence after K has been normalized to the interval [0,1].

## Classification

FHQCM theoretical construct.

## Scientific Integrity Note

Quantum coherence and decoherence have precise technical meanings in physics. In FHQCM, coherence and decoherence are used philosophically unless explicitly stated otherwise.
