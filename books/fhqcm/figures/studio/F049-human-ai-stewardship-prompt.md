# Figure Studio Prompt: F049-human-ai-stewardship

## Title
F049 — Human-AI Stewardship

## Caption
Human wisdom and AI capability are shown in reciprocal service under ethical stewardship.

## Context
AI Stewardship and Participatory Intelligence Purpose This chapter connects the FHQCM to AI Freedom Trust, Booksmith AI, author sovereignty, local-first tools, provenance, human judgment, and the ethical role of artificial intelligence as a stewarded capability rather than a replacement for wisdom. AI is treated here neither as savior nor as enemy. It is treated as a powerful participatory instrument that must be governed by human agency, transparency, accountability, and love. F049 — Human-AI StewardshipHuman wisdom and AI capability are shown in reciprocal service under ethical stewardship.F049-human-ai-stewardship From Computation to Stewardship The modern question of machine intelligence begins with computation, formal systems, and the question of whether machines can perform tasks associated with human thought turing1950ComputingMachinery. Later AI research developed symbolic systems, statistical learning, and increasingly powerful models of language and pattern recognition russellNorvig2020AI. FHQCM does not treat intelligence as merely calculation. Intelligence is relational: it depends on context, meaning, memory, feedback, purpose, and responsibility. This means AI systems must be evaluated not only by what they can generate, but by what kinds of relationships they create. The Alignment Problem AI alignment asks whether artificial systems can reliably act in ways compatible with human values, safety, and flourishing. Stuart Russell's work frames this as a problem of control, uncertainty, and human-compatible design russell2019HumanCompatible. Bostrom's work highlights the civilizational stakes of advanced AI systems bostrom2014Superintelligence. The FHQCM contribution is to insist that alignment cannot be reduced to technical optimization alone. A system may optimize while still fragmenting human agency, privacy, authorship, truth, or community. Therefore alignment must include sovereignty, provenance, inspectability, and consent.
