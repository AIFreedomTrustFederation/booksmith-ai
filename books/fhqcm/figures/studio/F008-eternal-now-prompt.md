## Title
F008 — Eternal Now

## Caption
Past, present, future, and the timeless center are represented as a unified field rather than a merely linear sequence.

## Context
In ordinary language, a coincidence is the occurrence of two or more events that appear meaningfully related yet lack an immediately obvious causal connection. The term derives from the Latin coincidere, meaning ``to fall together.'' Events are said to coincide when they arrive at the same place, time, or meaning. Yet this common definition presupposes separation. It assumes that events normally exist apart and only occasionally intersect. This chapter explores an alternative hypothesis: coincidence is not the exception but the disclosure of a deeper coherence already present within the structure of reality. The Eternal Now The Eternal Now is the philosophical and theological proposition that all moments participate within a deeper unity not reducible to sequential clock time. In the FHQCM, the Eternal Now is not presented as an established physical law. It is an interpretive framework informed by theology, philosophy of time, process thought, relativity-inspired spacetime reflection, and consciousness studies.
