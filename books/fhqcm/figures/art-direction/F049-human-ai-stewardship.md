Later AI research developed symbolic systems, statistical learning, and increasingly powerful models of language and pattern recognition russellNorvig2020AI. FHQCM does not treat intelligence as merely calculation. Intelligence is relational: it depends on context, meaning, memory, feedback, purpose, and responsibility. This means AI systems must be evaluated not only by what they can generate, but by what kinds of relationships they create. The Alignment Problem AI alignment asks whether artificial systems can reliably act in ways compatible with human values, safety, and flourishing. Stuart Russell's work frames this as a problem of control, uncertainty, and human-compatible design russell2019HumanCompatible. Bostrom's work highlights the civilizational stakes of advanced AI systems bostrom2014Superintelligence. The FHQCM contribution is to insist that alignment cannot be reduced to technical optimization alone. A system may optimize while still fragmenting human agency, privacy, authorship, truth, or community. Therefore alignment must include sovereignty, provenance, inspectability, and consent.
