# FHQCM Canon Definitions

## Fractal-Holographic Quantum Consciousness Model (FHQCM)

A developing interdisciplinary framework that interprets reality as recursive, participatory, holographic, fractal, and consciousness-involved. It integrates scientific inspiration, philosophical synthesis, and spiritual interpretation while requiring careful editorial separation between those categories.

## Fractal Time

A model of time in which moments are treated as recursively related rather than isolated points in a strict linear sequence. In FHQCM, each moment may be read as containing echoes of past and future patterns.

## Eternal Now

The metaphysical and theological frame in which past, present, and future are held together within a larger field of divine or cosmic awareness. This is not identical to any single physics theory of time; it is an interpretive bridge between spirituality, philosophy, and temporal models.

## Spiral Torus Field

A symbolic and geometric model of self-organizing flow, return, circulation, expansion, contraction, and recursive becoming. It is used to represent the movement of energy, matter, information, breath, consciousness, and cosmic process.

## Christic Consciousness

The model's principle of harmonic reconciliation. Christic Consciousness represents unity without erasing distinction, the reconciliation of opposites, and the restoration of divided realities into coherent relationship.

## Christic Equivalency

The symbolic use of the equal sign (=) as a sign of reconciliation. It represents the bridge between wave and particle, spirit and matter, past and future, self and other, creation and evolution, death and resurrection.

## Holographic Consciousness

The idea that consciousness may function in a distributed, whole-in-each-part pattern. In the book, this must be distinguished from established holographic principles in physics and from Pribram-inspired theories of brain and memory.

## Panentheism

The view that God is present within all creation while also transcending creation. This differs from pantheism, which identifies God and the universe without sufficient distinction.

## Panpsychism

The philosophical view that consciousness, mind-like properties, or proto-experience may be fundamental or widely distributed in reality. The book should present this as a philosophical position, not as settled empirical fact.

## Sacred Interconnectedness

The ethical and spiritual claim that beings are not isolated objects but relational participants in a living whole. FHQCM uses this as a moral bridge between metaphysics, recovery, ecology, technology, and community.

## Harmonic Coherence

A state of ordered relationship in which parts remain distinct while resonating within a larger unity. Love, reconciliation, truth, and integration are treated as coherence-increasing forces.

## Fragmentation

A state of dividedness, alienation, dissonance, or separation from sacred interconnectedness. In theological and recovery language, fragmentation may manifest as trauma, resentment, fear, shame, addiction, or egoic isolation.

## Conscious Co-Creation

The claim that conscious beings participate in shaping reality through observation, interpretation, action, intention, and relationship. Strong empirical claims require verification and careful wording.

## Source Provenance

The documented chain by which a claim, passage, figure, or equation moves from source material into edited manuscript and publication. Booksmith AI should preserve this chain.
