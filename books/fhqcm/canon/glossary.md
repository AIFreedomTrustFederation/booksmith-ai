# FHQCM Glossary

This glossary is the public-facing companion to `definitions.md`. It should become alphabetized, expanded, and cited before publication.

## Alpha and Omega

A theological symbol for divine relationship to time as both origin and fulfillment.

## Bohmian Inspiration

The influence of David Bohm's language of wholeness and implicate order on the model's interpretation of hidden unity.

## Christic Equivalency

The principle that Christic Consciousness functions symbolically as the equal sign that reconciles divided realities.

## Conscious Co-Creation

Participation of conscious beings in the unfolding of reality through observation, action, meaning, and relationship.

## Decoherence

In physics, the process by which quantum systems lose observable coherence through interaction with an environment. In FHQCM, decoherence is also interpreted symbolically as the apparent collapse of unity into separateness.

## Eternal Now

A frame in which all temporal moments are understood as gathered into a deeper unity.

## Fractal

A self-similar structure or pattern that repeats across scale.

## Fractal Time

Time interpreted as recursively patterned rather than merely linear.

## Holographic Brain

A theory associated with Karl Pribram that memory and perception may involve distributed processing patterns. FHQCM uses this as inspiration for broader holographic consciousness language.

## Holographic Principle

In physics, the idea that information in a volume may be represented at a boundary. In the book this must be distinguished from metaphorical uses of holography.

## Harmonic Coherence

Integrated relationship among parts within a whole.

## Implicate Order

David Bohm's term for a deeper order from which explicate phenomena unfold.

## Observer Participation

The idea, associated especially with Wheeler-inspired interpretations, that observation plays a role in the articulation of phenomena.

## Panentheism

God within all things and beyond all things.

## Panpsychism

The philosophical view that consciousness or mind-like properties are fundamental or widely present in reality.

## Recursive Consciousness

Consciousness understood as self-reflective, self-similar, and capable of participating in its own unfolding.

## Spiral Torus Field

The toroidal symbol of circulation, self-organization, return, and becoming.

## STOEM

Spiritual Theory of Everything Model. A related Drive source was found and should be reviewed as comparative background, not automatically merged into FHQCM.

## Toroidal Recursion

The movement of flow from source, through manifestation, and back toward source while preserving transformation.
